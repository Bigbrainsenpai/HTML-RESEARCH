Chicken Adobo

The quintessential Filipino dish — chicken simmered in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and bay leaves until tender and deeply flavored.

Prep15 min + marinate Cook30 min DifficultyEasy Estimated CostModerate

Ingredients

  • chicken
  • soy sauce
  • vinegar
  • garlic
  • onion
  • bay leaves
  • whole peppercorn
  • cooking oil
  • water
  • salt
  • pepper

Steps

  1. Marinate chicken in soy sauce and garlic for at least 1 hour.
  2. Pan-fry the marinated chicken 1–2 minutes per side to brown it.
  3. Pour the marinade into the pot, add water, bay leaves, and peppercorn. Boil.
  4. Simmer 20–25 minutes until chicken is tender.
  5. Add vinegar. Do not stir — let it boil first to mellow the sourness.
  6. Reduce sauce until it thickens. Serve with steamed rice.

Chicken Inasal

Bacolod's famous grilled chicken marinated in calamansi, vinegar, garlic, and annatto oil — perfect with garlic rice and the signature basting sauce.

Prep15 min + 3h marinate Cook25 min DifficultyMedium Estimated CostModerate

Ingredients

  • chicken
  • calamansi juice
  • vinegar
  • garlic
  • ginger
  • lemongrass
  • sugar
  • salt
  • pepper
  • annatto oil
  • bamboo skewers

Steps

  1. Crush lemongrass, garlic, and ginger together.
  2. Mix with calamansi juice, vinegar, sugar, salt, and pepper to make the marinade.
  3. Add chicken and marinate overnight in the fridge (minimum 3 hours).
  4. Prepare basting sauce: annatto oil + margarine + salt.
  5. Grill chicken over charcoal or indoor grill on medium heat.
  6. Baste with annatto oil mixture every few minutes while grilling.
  7. Cook 10–15 minutes per side until slightly charred. Serve with garlic rice.

Chicken Tinola

A comforting ginger chicken soup with green papaya or sayote and leafy greens — classic Filipino comfort food.

Prep10 min Cook25 min DifficultyEasy Estimated CostLow

Ingredients

  • chicken
  • green papaya or sayote
  • ginger
  • garlic
  • onion
  • fish sauce
  • water
  • malunggay or chili leaves
  • cooking oil

Steps

  1. Sauté garlic, onion, and ginger in oil until onion softens.
  2. Add chicken pieces. Cook 5 minutes until lightly browned.
  3. Add fish sauce and stir.
  4. Pour in water (or rice washing). Bring to a boil.
  5. Simmer 20–25 minutes until chicken is tender.
  6. Add green papaya or sayote. Cook 5–7 minutes until tender.
  7. Add malunggay or chili leaves. Cover 2 minutes. Serve hot with rice.

Pork Barbecue

Skewered pork marinated in a sweet-savory blend and grilled over charcoal — a beloved street food staple at every Filipino gathering and fiesta.

Prep20 min Cook20 min DifficultyEasy Estimated CostModerate

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork shoulder, thinly sliced
  • ½ cup banana ketchup
  • ¼ cup soy sauce
  • ¼ cup calamansi juice
  • 3 tbsp brown sugar
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp salt
  • Bamboo skewers, soaked in water

Steps

  1. Mix ketchup, soy sauce, calamansi juice, brown sugar, garlic, pepper, and salt in a large bowl.
  2. Add pork slices and toss to coat thoroughly. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours.
  3. Thread marinated pork onto soaked bamboo skewers, folding each slice accordion-style.
  4. Preheat grill or charcoal to medium-high heat.
  5. Grill skewers for 3–4 minutes per side, basting with reserved marinade each flip.
  6. Cook until pork is slightly charred and fully cooked. Serve with garlic rice and banana ketchup.

Lechon Kawali

Crunchy pork belly with golden skin and succulent meat — fried to perfection and served with vinegar dip or liver sauce.

Prep15 min Cook55 min DifficultyMedium Estimated CostModerate

Ingredients

  • pork belly slab
  • garlic
  • bay leaves
  • salt
  • peppercorn
  • cooking oil (for deep frying)

Steps

  1. Place pork belly in a pot. Add garlic, bay leaves, salt, peppercorn, and enough water to cover.
  2. Boil then simmer 35–45 minutes until pork is tender.
  3. Remove pork and let it cool completely. Pat very dry.
  4. Heat oil in a deep pan over high heat.
  5. Deep-fry pork belly until the skin is golden and very crispy (about 10–15 min).
  6. Remove, drain oil, chop into pieces. Serve with vinegar dip or liver sauce.

Pork Sinigang

Tender pork ribs slow-cooked in a tangy tamarind broth with tomato, radish, eggplant, and kangkong — a comforting Filipino sour soup.

Prep10 min Cook40 min DifficultyEasy Estimated CostLow

Ingredients

  • pork ribs
  • tamarind soup mix
  • tomato
  • onion
  • radish
  • eggplant
  • kangkong
  • water
  • fish sauce

Steps

  1. Boil pork ribs in water. Skim the scum off the top.
  2. Add onion and tomatoes. Simmer 30–40 minutes until pork is tender.
  3. Add tamarind soup mix and stir. Taste and adjust sourness.
  4. Add radish and eggplant. Cook 5 minutes.
  5. Add kangkong leaves last. Cook 2 minutes.
  6. Season with fish sauce. Serve hot with rice.

Beef Caldereta

A rich beef stew in tomato sauce with liver spread and bell peppers — hearty, savory, and perfect for celebrations.

Prep20 min Cook60 min DifficultyMedium Estimated CostModerate

Ingredients

  • beef chunks
  • tomato sauce
  • liver spread
  • potato
  • carrot
  • red and green bell pepper
  • garlic
  • onion
  • water
  • salt
  • pepper

Steps

  1. Sauté garlic and onion until fragrant.
  2. Add beef chunks. Cook until browned on all sides.
  3. Pour in tomato sauce and enough water to cover beef.
  4. Simmer 45–60 minutes until beef is tender.
  5. Add potatoes and carrots. Cook until soft.
  6. Stir in liver spread and bell peppers.
  7. Cook until sauce thickens. Season with salt and pepper. Serve with rice.

Beef Tapa

Thinly sliced beef marinated in soy and calamansi, then pan-fried until caramelized — a classic Filipino breakfast favorite.

Prep10 min + overnight Cook10 min DifficultyEasy Estimated CostModerate

Ingredients

  • beef sirloin, thinly sliced
  • soy sauce
  • calamansi juice
  • garlic
  • sugar
  • salt
  • pepper
  • cooking oil

Steps

  1. Mix soy sauce, calamansi juice, garlic, sugar, salt, and pepper in a bowl.
  2. Add beef slices. Mix well. Marinate overnight in the fridge.
  3. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat.
  4. Fry beef slices in batches — don't overcrowd the pan.
  5. Cook until slightly caramelized and edges are a little charred.
  6. Serve with garlic fried rice and fried egg (classic tapsilog).

Sinigang na Hipon

A bright and tangy shrimp soup loaded with vegetables — quick, refreshing, and perfect with steamed rice.

Prep10 min Cook15 min DifficultyEasy Estimated CostLow

Ingredients

  • shrimp (whole, unpeeled)
  • tamarind soup mix
  • onion
  • tomato
  • radish
  • eggplant
  • kangkong
  • water
  • fish sauce

Steps

  1. Boil water with onion and tomatoes for 5 minutes.
  2. Add tamarind soup mix. Stir and taste the sourness.
  3. Add radish. Cook 3 minutes.
  4. Add eggplant. Cook 3 more minutes.
  5. Add shrimp last — they cook fast, only 3–5 minutes.
  6. Add kangkong. Season with fish sauce. Serve immediately with rice.

Inihaw na Bangus

Grilled milkfish stuffed with tomato, onion, and ginger — smoky, tangy, and served with calamansi dipping sauce.

Prep15 min Cook18 min DifficultyEasy Estimated CostLow

Ingredients

  • whole bangus (milkfish)
  • tomato
  • onion
  • ginger
  • calamansi
  • salt
  • pepper
  • cooking oil

Steps

  1. Clean the bangus. Butterfly it open (or ask the fish vendor to do this).
  2. Season the inside with salt and pepper.
  3. Stuff with diced tomato, onion, and sliced ginger.
  4. Rub outside with oil and a little salt.
  5. Wrap in foil or grill directly over charcoal.
  6. Grill 8–10 minutes per side until cooked through.
  7. Serve with soy sauce + calamansi dipping sauce.
