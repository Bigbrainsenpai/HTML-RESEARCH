Chicken Adobo

The quintessential Filipino dish — braised in a tangy, savory blend of vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and bay leaves until fall-off-the-bone tender.

Prep15 min Cook45 min DifficultyEasy Estimated Cost??????

Ingredients

  • onions
  • garlic
  • chicken
  • salt
  • ground black pepper
  • soy sauce
  • hot water
  • dried laurel leaves
  • whole black peppercorn
  • cooking oil
  • vinegar

Steps

  1. Sauté onions and garlic on high heat
  2. Add chicken, season, and cook until fat renders
  3. Add soy sauce and reduce on high heat
  4. Pour in hot water, add laurel leaves and peppercorn, then simmer
  5. Separate chicken and pan-fry in oil
  6. Return sauce, add vinegar and a bit of water
  7. Simmer and reduce sauce until desired consistency

Pork Barbecue

Skewered pork marinated in a sweet-savory blend and grilled over charcoal — a beloved street food staple at every Filipino gathering and fiesta.

Prep20 min Cook20 min DifficultyEasy Estimated Cost??????

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork shoulder, thinly sliced
  • ½ cup banana ketchup
  • ¼ cup soy sauce
  • ¼ cup calamansi juice
  • 3 tbsp brown sugar
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp salt
  • Bamboo skewers, soaked in water

Steps

  1. Mix ketchup, soy sauce, calamansi juice, brown sugar, garlic, pepper, and salt in a large bowl.
  2. Add pork slices and toss to coat thoroughly. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours.
  3. Thread marinated pork onto soaked bamboo skewers, folding each slice accordion-style.
  4. Preheat grill or charcoal to medium-high heat.
  5. Grill skewers for 3–4 minutes per side, basting with reserved marinade each flip.
  6. Cook until pork is slightly charred and fully cooked. Serve with garlic rice and banana ketchup.
