Halo-Halo

The ultimate Filipino shaved ice dessert layered with sweetened beans, fruits, jellies, leche flan, and ube ice cream — a colorful summer celebration in a glass.

PrepN/A CookN/A DifficultyEasy Estimated Cost₱80–₱150

Ingredients

  • Shaved ice
  • Kaong (sugar palm fruit)
  • Boiled kidney beans
  • Pinipig (flat rice crisps)
  • Nata de coco
  • Ripe jackfruit
  • Colored gelatin
  • Tapioca pearls
  • Sweetened plantains (minatamis na saging)
  • Macapuno
  • Sugar
  • Evaporated milk
  • Leche flan (topping)
  • Ube halaya (topping)

Steps

  1. Place all sweetened ingredients (kaong, beans, pinipig, nata de coco, jackfruit, gelatin, tapioca, plantains, macapuno) into a tall glass with a few teaspoons of sugar.
  2. Pack shaved ice on top.
  3. Top with leche flan and ube halaya (or ice cream).
  4. Pour evaporated milk over everything.
  5. Stir gently to distribute before eating.

Leche Flan

A silky, rich custard made with egg yolks and condensed milk, topped with golden caramel — the crown jewel of Filipino desserts at every celebration.

Prep10 mins Cook45 mins DifficultyMedium Estimated Cost₱100–₱150

Ingredients

  • 10 eggs (yolks only)
  • 1 can (14 oz) condensed milk
  • 1 cup fresh milk or evaporated milk
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Steps

  1. Separate egg yolks from whites. Beat yolks in a bowl.
  2. Add condensed milk and mix thoroughly.
  3. Pour in fresh milk and vanilla extract. Mix well.
  4. Place the llanera (mold) on the stove over low heat. Add granulated sugar and stir until it melts into light brown caramel.
  5. Spread the caramel evenly on the flat side of the mold. Wait 5 minutes.
  6. Pour the egg and milk mixture into the mold. Cover with aluminum foil.
  7. Steam for 30–35 minutes.
  8. Let cool, then refrigerate. Serve chilled.

Turon

Crispy fried banana spring rolls coated in caramelized sugar — a beloved Filipino street food snack that doubles as a dessert.

Prep10 mins Cook12 mins DifficultyEasy Estimated Cost₱50–₱80

Ingredients

  • 6 pieces saba bananas (plantains), cut in half lengthwise
  • 1 cup ripe jackfruit, sliced
  • 1½ cups sugar
  • 12 pieces lumpia wrappers
  • 2 cups cooking oil

Steps

  1. Roll each banana half in sugar until well coated.
  2. Place sugared banana on a lumpia wrapper. Add a slice of jackfruit on top.
  3. Fold and roll the wrapper tightly. Seal the edge with water.
  4. Heat oil in a pan. Add some sugar to the hot oil and wait until it floats.
  5. Fry the wrapped bananas until the wrapper turns golden brown and the caramelized sugar sticks to the outside.
  6. Serve hot as a dessert or snack.

Bibingka

A traditional Filipino rice cake baked in clay pots lined with banana leaves, topped with salted eggs and grated coconut — a Christmas morning staple.

Prep10 mins Cook35 mins DifficultyMedium Estimated Cost₱120–₱180

Ingredients

  • 1 cup rice flour
  • ⅛ tsp salt
  • 2½ tsp baking powder
  • 3 tbsp butter
  • ½ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • ¼ cup fresh milk
  • 1 piece salted duck egg, sliced
  • ½ cup grated cheese
  • 3 raw eggs
  • ¼ cup grated coconut
  • Pre-cut banana leaf

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F.
  2. Combine rice flour, baking powder, and salt. Mix and set aside.
  3. Cream butter, gradually adding sugar while whisking.
  4. Add eggs and whisk until incorporated.
  5. Gradually add the flour mixture, then pour in coconut milk and fresh milk. Whisk for 1–2 minutes.
  6. Line a baking pan with pre-cut banana leaf. Pour in the batter.
  7. Bake for 15 minutes. Remove from oven and top with sliced salted egg and grated cheese (keep oven on).
  8. Return to oven and bake another 15–20 minutes until the top is medium brown.
  9. Remove, let cool, brush with butter, and top with grated coconut. Serve.

Puto

Fluffy steamed rice cakes that are subtly sweet and soft — often topped with salted egg or cheese, and always present at Filipino gatherings.

PrepN/A Cook15 mins DifficultyMedium Estimated Cost₱100–₱160

Ingredients

  • Asado Filling:
  • 1 lb pork, sliced into small pieces
  • 1 tsp Knorr Liquid Seasoning
  • 1 onion, minced
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 pieces star anise
  • ¼ cup brown sugar
  • 3 tbsp cooking oil
  • 2 tsp cornstarch
  • Ground black pepper to taste
  • Puto Batter:
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups cake flour
  • 2½ tbsp baking powder
  • 1½ cups sugar
  • ½ cup butter, melted
  • 1 egg
  • 1 cup evaporated milk
  • 2 cups water
  • Sliced salted duck egg (for topping)

Steps

  1. Make filling: Sauté onion in oil, add pork and cook until lightly brown. Pour in soy sauce and water. Boil, then add star anise. Cook on low for 30–35 minutes. Add brown sugar, seasoning, and pepper. Thicken with cornstarch dissolved in 3 tbsp water. Set aside.
  2. Make batter: Combine flours, sugar, and baking powder in a bowl. Add egg, evaporated milk, water, and melted butter. Mix until smooth.
  3. Pour 1 tbsp batter into each greased mold. Add 1–1½ tsp asado filling. Cover with more batter until ¾ full.
  4. Steam for 2–3 minutes, then top with a slice of salted duck egg. Continue steaming for 13–15 minutes.
  5. Let cool, remove from molds, and serve.

Ube Halaya

A thick, sweet purple yam jam made with condensed milk and butter — a vibrant and iconic Filipino dessert spread used in pastries, cakes, and halo-halo.

Prep12 mins Cook30 mins DifficultyMedium Estimated Cost₱120–₱180

Ingredients

  • 1 lb grated purple yam (raw)
  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • ¾ cup granulated white sugar
  • 1 tsp cooking oil

Steps

  1. Pour coconut milk into a cooking pot and bring to a boil.
  2. Once boiling, add sugar and stir until fully dissolved.
  3. Add grated purple yam. Cook over low to medium heat, stirring continuously, until the mixture becomes thick (about 20–30 minutes).
  4. Grease a llanera with cooking oil. Transfer the ube halaya into the mold.
  5. Let cool to room temperature, then refrigerate covered with cling wrap.
  6. Serve chilled.

Buko Pandan

A chilled dessert salad of young coconut strips and green pandan jelly in sweet cream — refreshing, fragrant, and a crowd favorite at parties.

Prep30 mins Cook15 mins + 8 hrs DifficultyEasy Estimated Cost₱130–₱200

Ingredients

  • 20 oz young coconut strips
  • 250 ml Nestlé All-Purpose Cream
  • 150 ml Nestlé Carnation Condensada (condensed milk)
  • ½ cup cooked sago pearls (optional)
  • 2 cups water
  • 3 oz powdered gelatin
  • ½ lb pandan leaves
  • ¼ cup sugar
  • ½ tsp buko pandan flavoring

Steps

  1. Boil water in a saucepan. Add pandan leaves, cover, and boil for 15 minutes. Remove leaves.
  2. Add sugar, powdered gelatin, and buko pandan flavoring to the pandan water. Stir until fully dissolved.
  3. Turn off heat. Pour into a mold and allow to cool until firm. Refrigerate for faster setting.
  4. Combine young coconut strips, condensed milk, all-purpose cream, and cooked sago pearls in a bowl. Chill for a few hours until texture thickens.
  5. Slice the firm gelatin into ½-inch cubes. Mix into the creamy coconut mixture.
  6. Transfer to serving cups. Optionally top with a scoop of vanilla ice cream. Serve chilled.

Cassava Cake

A dense, chewy baked dessert made from grated cassava, coconut milk, and condensed milk — moist, sweet, and topped with a creamy custard layer.

Prep15 mins Cook1 hr DifficultyMedium Estimated Cost₱150–₱220

Ingredients

  • Batter:
  • 2 lbs grated cassava
  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 6 oz evaporated milk
  • 3 eggs
  • ¼ cup butter, melted
  • 6 tbsp cheddar cheese, grated
  • ½ cup condensed milk
  • 14 tbsp granulated white sugar
  • Topping:
  • 2 tbsp flour
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • ½ cup condensed milk
  • 2 tbsp cheddar cheese, grated
  • 2 cups coconut milk

Steps

  1. Combine grated cassava, butter, condensed milk, evaporated milk, 6 tbsp cheese, sugar, and 2 eggs in a bowl. Mix well. Add coconut milk and mix again.
  2. Grease a baking pan. Pour in batter. Preheat oven to 350°F for 10 minutes, then bake for 1 hour. Remove and set aside.
  3. Make topping: Combine sugar and flour in a heated saucepan. Add condensed milk, mix. Add cheese and stir. Pour in coconut milk and stir constantly for 10 minutes.
  4. Pour topping over the baked cassava and spread evenly.
  5. Separate the yolk from the remaining egg. Brush the egg white glaze over the topping.
  6. Set oven to broil. Broil until top turns light brown. Garnish with extra grated cheese. Serve.

Sapin-Sapin

Layered sticky rice cake in purple, white, and yellow — each layer flavored with ube, coconut, and jackfruit. A visually stunning kakanin.

Prep20 mins Cook45 mins DifficultyHard Estimated Cost₱180–₱250

Ingredients

  • 2 cups glutinous rice flour
  • 4 cups coconut milk
  • 1 cup granulated white sugar
  • 7 oz (½ can) condensed milk
  • ½ cup ripe jackfruit
  • ¾ cup mashed purple yam
  • ½ tsp vanilla extract
  • ¼ tsp ube extract
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil or coconut oil
  • Violet food coloring
  • Yellow food coloring
  • ¼ cup latik (toasted coconut curds)

Steps

  1. Combine glutinous rice flour and sugar in a bowl. Add condensed milk, coconut milk, and vanilla. Mix until smooth.
  2. Divide mixture into 3 equal parts.
  3. Mix the first part with mashed purple yam, ube extract, and violet food coloring.
  4. Process jackfruit in a food processor, then mix into the second part with yellow food coloring.
  5. Leave the third part plain (white).
  6. Grease a round baking pan with oil. Pour the violet layer in first. Cover with cheesecloth and steam for 12–16 minutes.
  7. Pour the white layer on top. Re-cover with cheesecloth (squeeze out excess water first). Steam another 12–16 minutes.
  8. Pour the yellow layer on top. Steam for 15–20 minutes until fully set.
  9. Line a serving plate with oiled banana leaf. Invert the pan onto it so the violet layer is on top.
  10. Brush oil on top and sprinkle with latik. Serve.

Pichi-Pichi

Soft, translucent steamed cassava balls coated in grated coconut — a simple yet addictive Filipino delicacy that's chewy, sweet, and fragrant.

Prep15 mins Cook45 mins DifficultyEasy Estimated Cost₱60–₱90

Ingredients

  • 2 cups grated cassava
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 cup grated coconut
  • ½ tsp lye water
  • ½ tsp buko-pandan essence

Steps

  1. Combine cassava, sugar, and water in a bowl. Mix well.
  2. Add lye water while continuously stirring.
  3. Add buko-pandan essence and mix again.
  4. Pour evenly into individual cup molds and place in a steamer.
  5. Steam for 45 minutes to 1 hour until the color turns translucent.
  6. Allow to cool for 40 minutes to 1 hour (or refrigerate after 15 minutes of cooling). Remove from molds.
  7. Roll each piece in grated coconut. Arrange on a serving plate and serve.
