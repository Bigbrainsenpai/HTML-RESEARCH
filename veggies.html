Pinakbet

A classic Ilocano vegetable stew sautéed with bagoong alamang — bitter melon, eggplant, okra, and squash come together in this iconic dish.

Prep15 mins Cook25 mins DifficultyEasy Estimated Cost₱120–₱180

Ingredients

  • 1 cup bitter melon (ampalaya), sliced
  • 1 cup eggplant, sliced
  • 1 cup okra, trimmed
  • 1 cup squash (kalabasa), cubed
  • 3 tbsp bagoong alamang
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a pan and sauté garlic, onion, and tomatoes until soft.
  2. Add bagoong alamang and cook for 2 minutes.
  3. Add squash first and pour in water. Cover and cook for 5 minutes.
  4. Add eggplant, okra, and bitter melon. Gently mix.
  5. Cover and simmer for 10–15 minutes until vegetables are tender but not mushy.
  6. Taste and adjust with bagoong as needed. Serve hot with rice.

Ginisang Monggo

Sautéed mung bean soup with garlic, onion, tomatoes, and malunggay leaves — a Friday tradition in many Filipino households.

Prep10 mins Cook40 mins DifficultyEasy Estimated Cost₱80–₱130

Ingredients

  • 1 cup mung beans (monggo), rinsed
  • 4 cups water
  • 100g pork belly or tinapa flakes (optional)
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 cup malunggay leaves
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce (patis)
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil

Steps

  1. Boil mung beans in 4 cups water for 25–30 minutes until soft and some beans have split. Set aside.
  2. In a separate pan, heat oil and sauté garlic, onion, and tomatoes.
  3. Add pork belly or tinapa and cook until lightly browned.
  4. Pour in the cooked monggo with its liquid. Stir to combine.
  5. Season with patis. Simmer for 5 minutes.
  6. Add malunggay leaves, stir, and cook for 2 more minutes. Serve hot.

Laing

Dried taro leaves slow-simmered in rich coconut milk with chili and shrimp paste — a Bicolano classic that's smoky, creamy, and deeply satisfying.

Prep10 mins Cook45 mins DifficultyMedium Estimated Cost₱100–₱160

Ingredients

  • 2 cups dried taro leaves (gabi leaves)
  • 2 cans (800ml) coconut milk
  • 3 tbsp bagoong alamang or shrimp paste
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 3–5 red chili peppers (siling labuyo)
  • 100g pork or shrimp (optional)
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a deep pan. Sauté garlic and onion until fragrant.
  2. Add shrimp paste and pork/shrimp. Cook for 3 minutes.
  3. Pour in coconut milk and bring to a simmer.
  4. Add dried taro leaves — do NOT stir yet. Let them absorb the coconut milk for 15 minutes.
  5. Add chili peppers. Gently stir and continue simmering for 20–25 minutes until the coconut milk reduces and oil separates.
  6. Taste and adjust seasoning. Serve with steamed rice.

Chop Suey

A colorful stir-fry of mixed vegetables with quail eggs and chicken or shrimp — the Filipino version that's a staple at every fiesta table.

Prep15 mins Cook15 mins DifficultyEasy Estimated Cost₱120–₱200

Ingredients

  • 1 cup cabbage, chopped
  • 1 cup cauliflower florets
  • 1 cup carrots, sliced
  • 1 cup snap peas or snow peas
  • 1 cup bell pepper, sliced
  • 12 pieces quail eggs, boiled and peeled
  • 200g chicken breast or shrimp
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 3 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch dissolved in 2 tbsp water
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a wok or large pan. Sauté garlic and onion.
  2. Add chicken or shrimp and cook until done.
  3. Add harder vegetables first (carrots, cauliflower) and stir-fry for 3 minutes.
  4. Add remaining vegetables and quail eggs. Toss to combine.
  5. Pour in chicken broth and oyster sauce. Season with salt and pepper.
  6. Add cornstarch mixture and stir until sauce thickens. Serve immediately.

Adobong Sitaw

String beans cooked adobo-style with vinegar, soy sauce, and garlic — simple, tasty, and a perfect everyday Filipino side dish.

Prep10 mins Cook20 mins DifficultyEasy Estimated Cost₱60–₱100

Ingredients

  • 300g string beans (sitaw), cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 3 tbsp white vinegar
  • ½ cup water
  • 1 tsp whole black peppercorns
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 100g pork belly (optional)
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a pan. Sauté garlic until golden. Add pork and cook until lightly browned.
  2. Add string beans and stir-fry for 2 minutes.
  3. Pour in soy sauce, vinegar, and water. Add peppercorns and bay leaves.
  4. Do not stir right after adding vinegar — let it cook uncovered for 3 minutes first.
  5. Stir, then cover and simmer for 10–12 minutes until beans are tender.
  6. Uncover and let sauce reduce slightly. Serve with rice.

Ginisang Ampalaya

Sautéed bitter melon with eggs, tomatoes, and onion — a nutritious and bold-flavored Filipino dish that's an acquired taste worth having.

Prep15 mins Cook15 mins DifficultyEasy Estimated Cost₱60–₱90

Ingredients

  • 2 medium bitter melons (ampalaya), sliced thin
  • 3 eggs, beaten
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce (patis) or soy sauce
  • 1 tsp salt (for soaking)
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil

Steps

  1. Slice ampalaya and rub with salt. Let sit for 10 minutes, then squeeze out the liquid to reduce bitterness. Rinse and set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a pan. Sauté garlic, onion, and tomatoes until softened.
  3. Add ampalaya and stir-fry for 3–4 minutes.
  4. Season with patis or soy sauce.
  5. Pour in beaten eggs and gently fold into the vegetables until just set.
  6. Serve immediately with steamed rice.

Ginataang Kalabasa

Squash and string beans simmered in creamy coconut milk with shrimp — a rich, comforting stew that pairs beautifully with steamed rice.

Prep10 mins Cook25 mins DifficultyEasy Estimated Cost₱120–₱170

Ingredients

  • 2 cups squash (kalabasa), cubed
  • 1 cup string beans (sitaw), cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 200g shrimp, shelled
  • 2 cans (800ml) coconut milk
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 tbsp bagoong alamang or fish sauce
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a pan. Sauté garlic and onion until fragrant.
  2. Add shrimp and cook until pink. Remove and set aside.
  3. Pour in coconut milk and bring to a gentle boil.
  4. Add squash and cook for 8–10 minutes until tender.
  5. Add string beans and bagoong. Simmer for 5 more minutes.
  6. Return shrimp to the pan. Season with salt and pepper. Serve hot.

Ensaladang Talong

Grilled eggplant salad with tomatoes, onions, and bagoong — a smoky, tangy side dish that's light and refreshing alongside fried or grilled meats.

Prep10 mins Cook15 mins DifficultyEasy Estimated Cost₱50–₱80

Ingredients

  • 3 medium eggplants (talong)
  • 2 medium tomatoes, diced
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 3 tbsp bagoong alamang
  • 2 tbsp vinegar (optional, for dressing)
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Grill eggplants directly over an open flame or grill pan, turning occasionally, until the skin is charred and the flesh is soft (about 10–12 minutes).
  2. Transfer to a plate and let cool slightly. Peel off the charred skin.
  3. Flatten the eggplant flesh with a fork on a plate.
  4. Top with diced tomatoes and onions.
  5. Spoon bagoong over the top. Drizzle with vinegar if desired.
  6. Serve immediately as a side dish.

Ginataang Langka

Young jackfruit simmered in coconut milk with bagoong and chili — tender, savory, and deeply satisfying as a main or side dish.

Prep15 mins Cook35 mins DifficultyMedium Estimated Cost₱100–₱150

Ingredients

  • 2 cups young jackfruit (langka), cut into chunks
  • 2 cans (800ml) coconut milk
  • 3 tbsp bagoong alamang
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 3–5 red chili peppers (siling labuyo)
  • 100g pork belly (optional)
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil

Steps

  1. Boil jackfruit pieces in salted water for 10 minutes to soften slightly. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat oil in a pan. Sauté garlic, onion, and pork until lightly browned.
  3. Add bagoong alamang and cook for 2 minutes.
  4. Pour in coconut milk and bring to a simmer.
  5. Add jackfruit and chili peppers. Stir gently.
  6. Simmer uncovered for 20–25 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the jackfruit is very tender and the sauce thickens. Serve with rice.

Pakbet Ilocano

The original Ilocano version of Pinakbet — less saucy, with more bagoong and a stronger fermented shrimp paste flavor that's unmistakably Ilocos.

Prep15 mins Cook20 mins DifficultyEasy Estimated Cost₱120–₱180

Ingredients

  • 1 cup bitter melon (ampalaya), sliced
  • 1 cup eggplant, sliced
  • 1 cup okra, trimmed
  • 1 cup squash (kalabasa), cubed
  • 1 cup string beans (sitaw)
  • 4 tbsp Ilocano bagoong (fermented fish/shrimp paste — stronger variety)
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 100g pork belly (optional)
  • ½ cup water
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a clay pot or heavy pan. Sauté garlic, onion, and pork until browned.
  2. Add bagoong and cook for 2 minutes, letting the flavor bloom.
  3. Add the firmer vegetables first — squash, eggplant, and string beans. Pour in water.
  4. Cover and cook for 8 minutes without stirring (this is key for Ilocano pakbet — avoid over-stirring).
  5. Add okra and ampalaya on top. Cover and cook for another 5–7 minutes.
  6. Gently shake the pan instead of stirring to keep vegetables intact. Serve hot.
