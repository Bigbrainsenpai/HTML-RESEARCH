Sago't Gulaman

A classic Filipino cold drink with tapioca pearls and cubes of grass jelly in a sweet brown sugar syrup — refreshing and beloved at every Filipino occasion.

Prep5 minutes Cook25 minutes DifficultyEasy Estimated Cost₱50–₱80

Ingredients

  • 1 cup tapioca pearls
  • ½ oz red gelatin powder (unflavored)
  • 6 cups water (for boiling)
  • 2 tbsp granulated white sugar
  • 1 cup brown sugar (for syrup)
  • 1 cup water (for syrup)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract (for syrup)

Steps

  1. Boil 4 cups of water, add tapioca pearls, and cook for 8 minutes on medium heat. Reduce to low and continue until pearls turn translucent. Set aside.
  2. Boil 2 cups of water, add white sugar and gelatin powder. Stir and turn off heat. Transfer to a glass container and refrigerate until set.
  3. Make the syrup by combining brown sugar, 1 cup water, and vanilla extract in a pot. Boil for 1 minute while stirring. Set aside.
  4. In a glass, combine tapioca pearls, sliced gelatin, 3 tbsp of syrup, and 1 cup cold water. Stir.
  5. Serve and enjoy!

Buko Juice

Fresh young coconut water served chilled with tender coconut meat strips — the most natural and hydrating Filipino drink straight from the tropical source.

Prep10 minutes CookNone DifficultyEasy Estimated Cost₱40–₱60

Ingredients

  • 1–2 young coconuts (buko)
  • Coconut water
  • Coconut meat
  • Ice cubes
  • 1–2 tbsp sugar (optional)

Steps

  1. Open the young coconut carefully.
  2. Pour the coconut water into a glass or pitcher.
  3. Scrape the soft coconut meat from the shell.
  4. Add the coconut meat into the juice.
  5. Add sugar if desired.
  6. Add ice cubes and serve chilled.

Calamansi Juice

Fresh-squeezed Filipino citrus juice sweetened with sugar and served over ice — tangy, bright, and the go-to thirst quencher under the tropical sun.

Prep10 minutes CookNone DifficultyEasy Estimated Cost₱20–₱40

Ingredients

  • 15–20 calamansi
  • ½ cup sugar
  • 3 cups cold water
  • Ice cubes

Steps

  1. Cut calamansi in half.
  2. Squeeze the juice into a bowl.
  3. Strain the juice to remove seeds.
  4. Add sugar and mix until dissolved.
  5. Add cold water and stir.
  6. Serve with ice.

Salabat

A warm and soothing Filipino ginger tea sweetened with brown sugar — the classic home remedy for colds, sore throats, and cold rainy days.

Prep5 minutes Cook10 minutes DifficultyEasy Estimated Cost₱20–₱30

Ingredients

  • 1 thumb-size ginger (sliced or crushed)
  • 2 cups water
  • 1–2 tbsp brown sugar or honey
  • Lemon or calamansi (optional)

Steps

  1. Boil water in a pot.
  2. Add sliced ginger.
  3. Simmer for about 10 minutes.
  4. Add sugar or honey.
  5. Strain the ginger pieces.
  6. Serve hot.

Avocado Shake

Creamy blended avocado with evaporated milk and sugar — a thick, rich Filipino shake that is sweeter and creamier than any version you've had before.

Prep5 minutes CookNone DifficultyEasy Estimated Cost₱50–₱70

Ingredients

  • 1 ripe avocado
  • 1 cup milk or evaporated milk
  • 2 tbsp sugar or condensed milk
  • Ice cubes

Steps

  1. Cut the avocado and remove the seed.
  2. Scoop the avocado flesh into a blender.
  3. Add milk and sugar.
  4. Add ice cubes.
  5. Blend until smooth.
  6. Serve chilled.

Pineapple Juice

Freshly squeezed or blended Philippine pineapple juice — sweet, tart, and absolutely refreshing, especially when served ice-cold at fiestas.

Ingredients

Melon Juice

Freshly blended cantaloupe melon with milk and sugar — a light, sweet, and subtly fragrant Filipino juice that tastes like a tropical afternoon.

Prep15 minutes Cook1 minute DifficultyEasy Estimated Cost₱60–₱90

Ingredients

  • 1 small cantaloupe
  • ¾ to 1 cup granulated white sugar
  • 10 to 12 cups water
  • ½ tsp vanilla extract
  • Ice cubes

Steps

  1. Slice the cantaloupe in half and remove all seeds.
  2. Using a melon baller or scraper, scrape the cantaloupe flesh into a large bowl.
  3. In a large pitcher, combine water, sugar, and vanilla extract. Stir until sugar dissolves.
  4. Add the shredded cantaloupe and stir.
  5. Add lots of ice and serve.

Buko Pandan Drink

A chilled drink version of the classic buko pandan — coconut water with young coconut strips and pandan jelly, lightly sweetened and utterly refreshing.

Prep15 minutes Cook10 minutes DifficultyMedium Estimated Cost₱120–₱180

Ingredients

  • 1 cup young coconut, shredded
  • 1 cup nata de coco
  • 1 pack pandan jelly
  • 1 cup all-purpose cream
  • ½ cup condensed milk
  • 3 cups cold water
  • Ice cubes

Steps

  1. Cook the pandan jelly according to package instructions.
  2. Let it cool and cut into cubes.
  3. In a bowl, combine shredded buko and nata de coco.
  4. Add pandan jelly cubes.
  5. Pour cream and condensed milk.
  6. Add cold water and mix well.
  7. Serve chilled.

Iced Milo

Cold chocolate malt drink made with Milo powder, chilled milk, and ice — a Filipino household staple that's been a childhood favorite for generations.

Prep3 minutes CookNone DifficultyVery Easy Estimated Cost₱15–₱25

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp Milo powder
  • 1 cup cold milk or water
  • Ice cubes
  • Extra Milo powder for topping (optional)

Steps

  1. Put Milo powder in a glass.
  2. Add cold milk or water.
  3. Stir until dissolved.
  4. Add ice cubes.
  5. Sprinkle extra Milo powder on top if desired.
  6. Serve immediately.
