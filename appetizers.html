Lumpiang Shanghai

Crispy deep-fried spring rolls filled with seasoned ground pork, carrots, and onions — the most popular Filipino party appetizer, always served with sweet chili sauce.

Prep25 mins Cook15 mins DifficultyEasy Estimated Cost₱150–₱250

Ingredients

  • Ground pork
  • Carrots (finely chopped)
  • Onion (finely chopped)
  • Garlic (minced)
  • Egg
  • Soy sauce
  • Salt and pepper
  • Lumpia wrappers
  • Cooking oil

Steps

  1. Mix pork, carrots, onion, garlic, egg, soy sauce, salt, and pepper.
  2. Place filling on wrapper and roll tightly.
  3. Seal edges and repeat.
  4. Heat oil and fry until golden brown.
  5. Drain and serve.

Lumpiang Sariwa

Fresh spring rolls wrapped in a soft crepe-like wrapper filled with sautéed vegetables, heart of palm, and topped with a sweet peanut sauce.

Prep30 mins Cook20 mins DifficultyMedium Estimated Cost₱200–₱350

Ingredients

  • Lumpia wrapper (soft)
  • Pork
  • Shrimp (optional)
  • Garlic
  • Onion
  • Carrots
  • Cabbage
  • Green beans
  • Sweet potato
  • Soy sauce
  • Oyster sauce
  • Peanut sauce
  • Lettuce

Steps

  1. Sauté garlic and onion, add pork and shrimp.
  2. Add vegetables and cook.
  3. Season with soy and oyster sauce.
  4. Place filling on wrapper with lettuce.
  5. Roll and top with peanut sauce.

Tokwa't Baboy

Crispy fried tofu and boiled pork ears tossed in a vinegar-soy dipping sauce with onions and chili — the ultimate Filipino pulutan for all occasions.

Prep20 mins Cook20 mins DifficultyEasy Estimated Cost₱150–₱250

Ingredients

  • Pork (boiled)
  • Firm tofu
  • Garlic
  • Onion
  • Soy sauce
  • Vinegar
  • Sugar
  • Chili peppers
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Boil pork and slice.
  2. Fry tofu and cut into cubes.
  3. Mix soy sauce, vinegar, sugar, garlic, and chili.
  4. Combine pork and tofu.
  5. Add sauce and mix well.

Kwek-Kwek

Hard-boiled quail eggs coated in an orange battered shell and deep-fried until crispy — a famous Filipino street food snack dipped in spiced vinegar.

Prep15 mins Cook10 mins DifficultyEasy Estimated Cost₱100–₱150

Ingredients

  • Quail eggs
  • Flour
  • Cornstarch
  • Water
  • Annatto powder
  • Salt
  • Cooking oil

Steps

  1. Boil and peel eggs.
  2. Make orange batter.
  3. Dip eggs in batter.
  4. Fry until crispy.
  5. Serve with vinegar sauce.

Fish Balls

Fried fish balls skewered on sticks, served with sweet sauce or spicy vinegar — the most iconic Filipino street food found on nearly every sidewalk cart.

Prep15 mins Cook10 mins DifficultyEasy Estimated Cost₱80–₱150

Ingredients

  • Sardines
  • Flour or cornstarch
  • Egg
  • Garlic
  • Onion
  • Salt and pepper
  • Cooking oil

Steps

  1. Mash sardines and mix ingredients.
  2. Form into balls.
  3. Fry until golden.
  4. Serve with sauce.

Dynamite Lumpia

Long green chili peppers stuffed with seasoned ground pork and cheese, wrapped in spring roll wrapper and deep-fried — spicy, cheesy, and dangerously addictive.

Prep25 mins Cook15 mins DifficultyMedium Estimated Cost₱150–₱250

Ingredients

  • Green chili peppers
  • Ground meat
  • Cheese
  • Garlic
  • Onion
  • Salt and pepper
  • Lumpia wrappers
  • Cooking oil

Steps

  1. Slice chili and remove seeds.
  2. Stuff with meat and cheese.
  3. Wrap in lumpia wrapper.
  4. Fry until crispy.
  5. Serve hot.

Kinilaw

Filipino-style ceviche — fresh raw fish cured in calamansi juice and vinegar with ginger, onion, and chili. The freshest appetizer from the Philippine coast.

Prep20 mins CookNo cook DifficultyEasy Estimated Cost₱200–₱350

Ingredients

  • Fresh fish
  • Vinegar
  • Calamansi juice
  • Ginger
  • Onion
  • Chili
  • Salt and pepper

Steps

  1. Cut fish into cubes.
  2. Wash in vinegar.
  3. Add calamansi, ginger, onion, and chili.
  4. Season and mix.
  5. Serve fresh.

Chicharon Bulaklak

Deep-fried pork mesentery (ruffled fat) until impossibly crispy — a bold and indulgent Filipino pulutan best enjoyed with spiced vinegar dip.

Prep30 mins Cook40 mins DifficultyMedium Estimated Cost₱200–₱300

Ingredients

  • Pork mesentery
  • Garlic
  • Bay leaves
  • Peppercorns
  • Salt
  • Cooking oil

Steps

  1. Clean and boil pork with spices.
  2. Drain and dry.
  3. Deep fry until crispy.
  4. Serve with vinegar dip.

  • Cornstarch
  • Egg

  • Salt and pepper

  2. Fry in hot oil.
  3. Cook until crispy.
  4. Serve with dipping sauce.
